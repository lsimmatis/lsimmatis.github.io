Publications

  • 2023
    3D Video Tracking Technology in the Assessment of Orofacial Impairments in Neurological Disease: Clinical Validation
    American Speech-Language-Hearing Association
    Demonstrated that facial movement features derived from a deep learning model can capture clinical phenomena in ALS and stroke.
  • 2023
    Analytical Validation of a Webcam-Based Assessment of Speech Kinematics: Digital Biomarker Evaluation following the V3 Framework
    S. Karger AG
    Validated a novel web-based speech assessment system. Demonstrated that digital speech assessment technologies have strong validity compared to lab-based systems, which will enable the use of digital health speech assessments in clinical applications.
  • 2022
    Concurrent Validity of Automatic Speech and Pause Measures During Passage Reading in ALS
    IEEE
    Demonstrated that Wav2Vec (a transformer-based ASR system) could outperform classical speech and pause estimation algorithms in dysarthric speech.
  • 2022
    Automated Temporal Segmentation of Orofacial Assessment Videos
    IEEE
    Found that deep learning-based movement repetition estimator was able to effectively segment videos of orofacial/speech assessments.
  • 2022
    Reliability of automatic computer vision-based assessment of orofacial kinematics for telehealth applications
    S. Karger AG
    Showed that deep learning-based facial movement features gathered remotely are as reliable as those gathered in a lab setting.
  • 2021
    Facial Landmark Tracking in Videos of Individuals with Neurological Impairments: Is There a Trade-off Between Smoothness and Accuracy?
    IEEE
    Demonstrated that fine tuning deep learning models optimized for smoothness or for accuracy resulted in a tradeoff in performance between better tracking at a frame level, or better tracking at a video level.
  • 2021
    Quantifying changes over 1 year in motor and cognitive skill after transient ischemic attack (TIA) using robotics.
    Nature
    Showed that people who had TIAs or migraines displayed atypical patterns of change on upper-limb robotic assessments for as much as 1-year post-event.
  • 2021
    Principal components analysis using data collected from healthy individuals on two robotic assessment platforms yields similar behavioral patterns.
    Frontiers
    Identified that exoskeleton and endpoint robotic systems capture similar patterns of upper-limb motor behaviour, validating the use of the simpler endpoint system.
  • 2020
    The feasibility of assessing cognitive and motor function in multiple sclerosis patients using robotics.
    BioMed Central
    Multiple sclerosis patients displayed mixtures of motor and cognitive impairments, which captured different patterns of disease severity than the standard MS assessment, the EDSS.
  • 2020
    Statistical measures of motor, sensory and cognitive performance across repeated robot-based testing.
    BioMed Central
    Features of upper-limb motor control gathered using a robot display favourable reliability and consistency properties.
  • 2020
    Assessing various sensorimotor and cognitive functions in people with epilepsy is feasible with robotics.
    Elsevier
    Patients with epilepsy displayed upper-limb sensorimotor impairments in motor and cognitive assessment domains, and the type/extent of impairment was related to the type of antiepileptic medications prescribed.
  • 2019
    The feasibility of using robotic technology to quantify sensory, motor, and cognitive impairments associated with ALS.
    Taylor & Francis
    Amyotrophic lateral sclerosis (ALS) is associated primarily with motor impairments, but here we demonstrate that it is also possible to identify cognitive-motor deficits over and above motor impairments.
  • 2019
    The impact of transient ischemic attack (TIA) on brain and behavior.
    Frontiers
    Transient ischaemic attack (TIA) is associated with a variety of motor and cognitive impairments; here, we reviewed the evidence that TIA, despite being a transient event, does not strictly have transient sequelae.
  • 2018
    Using principal component analysis to reduce complex datasets produced by robotic technology in healthy participants.
    BioMed Central
    We characterized patterns of upper limb motor control in a large cohort of health control participants, using data derived from the Kinarm Exoskeleton.
  • 2017
    Robotic exoskeleton assessment of transient ischemic attack.
    PLoS
    Transient ischaemic attack (TIA) is classically viewed as an event of little longterm consequence; however, we showed that up to 2 weeks after a TIA, patients had a variety of motor, sensory, and cognitive-motor deficits as determined using upper-limb robotic assessments.
